  • selection rates by demographic group
  • counterfactual score deltas with bootstrap confidence intervals

7. Summary and Key Findings

Main Findings

  1. Bias Exists: Counterfactual deltas are small (0.003-0.004) but statistically significant
  2. It Matters: Selection rate gaps of 7-8% at top-1 (embeddings), 17% (TF-IDF)
  3. Two Different Mechanisms:
    • TF-IDF: Strong selection bias with zero score deltas (structural/ranking bias)
    • Embeddings: Moderate selection bias with small score deltas (representation bias)
  4. Tokenization Effect: Black names fragment into 2+ subtokens (75% vs 0% for white names)
  5. Occupation Variation: Bias varies widely by job category (some occupations have no gap)

Statistical Methods

  • Bootstrap confidence intervals (2000 samples, 95% CI)
  • Multiple comparisons tested
  • Effect sizes reported (Cohen's d)
  • Subgroup analysis by occupation
